The Hawthorne Effect
Being observed changes behavior. Documented in 100+ studies. Visibility alone — no punishment needed — shifts what we do.
Dean Karlan, Yale
Commitments with social stakes are far more likely to be kept than private ones. Reputation outlasts willpower.

Every habit program that actually works is built on social accountability, not self-monitoring. The group is the mechanism.
Most people are Obligers — and that's completely normal
Gretchen Rubin's research found that Obligers — people who meet commitments made to others but struggle to meet their own — are the single largest personality group. Not a character flaw. Just how most people are wired. Togather is designed for exactly that.
